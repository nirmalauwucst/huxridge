Points to understand

The two main rates

Profits up to £50,000 are taxed at the 19% small profits rate. Profits of £250,000 or more are taxed at the 25% main rate.

Marginal relief

Between £50,000 and £250,000, tax is charged at 25% but reduced by marginal relief (fraction 3⁄200), giving a gradually rising effective rate.

Associated companies

The £50k and £250k thresholds are divided between the company and its associated companies, so profits can reach the higher rate sooner.

Short accounting periods

If your accounting period is shorter than 12 months, the thresholds are pro-rated for the number of days in the period.
